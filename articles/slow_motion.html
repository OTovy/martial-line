General Meditations about Martial Arts

The Slow-Motion Principle

Imagine someone is attacking or threatening you. Now imagine that the attacker is moving in extremely slow motion-exaggerated, like in the movies, really slow motion-while you are able to move at normal speed.

In this situation, if you act correctly, your chances of survival are quite high, and you will likely feel confident and capable. It might even seem somewhat amusing to you.

You could dodge, escape, or perhaps stop the attacker before they even have a chance to move, and they won’t be able to respond to you in time.

This scenario illustrates the Slow-Motion Principle, which states that the greater the difference between your speed and the attacker’s speed, the higher your chances of surviving.

Think of a person driving a car: if they maintain a safe distance and reduce their speed, their reaction time and ability to respond to road events increase, improving their chances of surviving dangerous situations.

There are two main ways to increase the speed difference and leverage this principle to your advantage:


Slowing Down the Attacker

  • Range management - Maintaining an appropriate distance and moving away.
  • Physical barrier - An object or obstacle between you and the attacker that forces them to deal with it.
  • Sensory disruption - Anything that causes sensory confusion for the attacker, forcing them to regain their bearings.
Speed Training
  • Perception speed - The ability to recognize threats early and on time, sharpening your senses and thinking.
  • Reaction speed - The ability to respond quickly and on time, the transition from recognizing to acting.
  • Movement speed - The ability to move your body quickly, including neuro-mechanical speed training and technique.
Each of these increases your time and improves your chances of delivering a rapid response that neutralizes the threat and ensures survival. Combining all of them together will leave the attacker ineffective and confused.
