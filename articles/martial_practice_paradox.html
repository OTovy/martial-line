General Meditations about Martial Arts

The Paradox of Martial Arts Practice

There is a fundamental contradiction in combat training, especially in anything dealing with fighting practicality.
Combat training is meant to teach how to act in situations of particularly high danger-high risk situations that threaten the fighter’s safety,
yet in order for the practice to take place, the training method must be one that keeps the trainee safe throughout the sessions,
and therefore can never reach the level of danger for which one is supposedly training.

From this we see that the very concept of "combat training" is an oxymoron.
Thus, the trainee never truly finds themselves practicing such situations, they always remain in speculative states of uncertainty, unless fate has it that they encounter (and survive) such a situation during their life.
And even then, that situation will differ from most other possible scenarios.

What training does provide, or at least is supposed to provide, is the sharpening of physical abilities and movement, sensory and emotional orientation, along with combative and survival senses.
This increases the chances of surviving dangerous self-defense situations.
However, these alone are not enough. In order for these abilities to transfer from the training environment to an environment of real high danger, there must be a set of tools that bridges the gap created by uncertainty and high entropy.
These tools are **intuition and intelligence**.

Practical training isn't merely a practice that teaches the most effective techniques for situations.
it is a practice that provides Orientation, Intelligence, and Intuition.
Everything else is a matter of Luck.
After all, fighting is a matter of probabilities, and combat training should be something that increases the probability of survival,
like playing poker, it's a game of skill in which one must wisely use those skills in situations of luck in order to increase the chances of winning.

What happens if you don’t even know you’re in the game?
It’s better to walk through the world holding a double-ace than a two-seven.
Good Luck.
